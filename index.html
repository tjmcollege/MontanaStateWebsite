The state of Montana was established in 1889, but was an established territory in 1864, and before that its lands were inhabited by the Crow, Cheyenne, Koontenai, Salish, and other native american tribes.

Montana's land, from mountainous forests in the west and large plains in the east, have provided bountiful resources for its inhabitants. In the west the Clark Fork and Bitteroot rivers flow into the pacific-bound Columbia river, and in the east you can find the watersheds of the mighty Missouri river.

The state economy in the past was focused on resource exploitation, from lumber to minerals to oil. Today the fastest growing industry is tourism as many want to experience what Montana has to offer.

Montana statistics:

  1. Montana is the 4th largest state in the union
  2. Montana has a population of 1,122,867 people
  3. The denonym for a person who is from Montana is Montanan
